The double-open review model seems natural when treating code and data. This is perhaps a consequence of the social norms in open-source-software communities. Open peer review of the manuscript itself still raises concerns, however. While there's some evidence that open reviews are of higher quality, early career researchers shy away from the idea of having to write a critical review of a senior researcher's work, fearing career retribution.
